Real Rockets in Kerbal Space Program

Delta V Heavy

Since the retirement of the Space Shuttle in 2011, the Delta V Heavy is now the largest capacity rocket in operation. At $400 million per launch, the Delta V heavy can get a payload of 28,790kg to LEO, using 628,420kg of propellant in the process.

Both the real rocket and the KSP replica use LH2 and LOX to fuel both stages, but unlike the real rocket, the replica includes fuel pumps leading from the side boosters to the core tank

Space Shuttle

The Space Shuttle is one of the most recognisable rockets in history, this particular image is of STS-120, flown by Space Shuttle Discorvery on 23/10/2007, carrying the Harmoney module (node 2) to the ISS.

In order to get Discorvery to the ISS, the Space Shuttle used 3 RS-25 engines fueled by LH2 and LOX, and two solid rocket boosteres attached on either side of the large orange tank. The KSP replica uses a white tank, just like the first two Shuttle missions. The white paint was removed after STS-2, increasing the payload capacity.

Space Shuttle (Orbiter)

On the left is a picture of the Space Shuttle Atlantis shortly before landing at the KSC following STS-122. On the right is the KSP replica, firing it's OME (Orbital Maneuvering Engines) as is prepares for its journey back home.

Atlas V 551

The Atlas V is a 2 stage rocket operated by ULA. The first stage uses one RD-180 engine, fueled by RP-1/LOX, while the second stage uses LH2/LOX to fuel its single RL10 engine.

Perhaps what is most interesting about the Atlas V is the configuration of its SRBs (Solid Rocket Boosters). As seen here, there SRBs are not symmetrical, which mean the thrust does not line up with the center of mass. This would cause the rocket to rotate, but the engine on the first stage can gimbal to cancel out this rotation.

Saturn 1B

The Saturn 1B played an important part in getting the first men to the moon. Its resemblance to the Saturn V is no coincident, much of the Saturn V harware was tested on the Saturn 1B, such as the CSM (Command/Service Module), the Lunar Module, and the S-IVB upper stage. It flew the Apollo 5 mission, and sent crew to Skylab, America's current space station.

Just the the real thing, the KSP replica has 8 engines in its first stage, burning RP-1/LOX, and a single engine burning LH2/LOX in the S-IVB upper stage. The replica can be seen taking the CSD to orbit, just like it did in real life.

Controlling rockets by hand itn't easy, but the mod kRPC allows you to write code in languages, such as Python, to conrol rockets in KSP.

Gravity turn

The program changes your pitch based uppon the rockets altitude, for every 500m gained in altitude, the pitch decreases by 1°, capped out at 0°.
