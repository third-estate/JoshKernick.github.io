Real Rockets in Kerbal Space Program

Delta V Heavy

Since the retirement of the Space Shuttle in 2011, the Delta V Heavy is now the largest capacity rocket in operation. At $400 million per launch, the Delta V heavy can get a payload of 28,790kg to LEO, using 628,420kg of propellant in the process.

Both the real rocket and the KSP replica use LH2 and LOX to fuel both stages, but unlike the real rocket, the replica includes fuel pumps leading from the side boosters to the core tank

Space Shuttle

The Space Shuttle is one of the most recognisable rockets in history, this particular image is of STS-120, flown by Space Shuttle Discorvery on 23/10/2007, carrying the Harmoney module (node 2) to the ISS.

In order to get Discorvery to the ISS, the Space Shuttle used 3 RS-25 engines fueled by LH2 and LOX, and two solid rocket boosteres attached on either side of the large orange tank. The KSP replica uses a white tank, just like the first two Shuttle missions. The white paint was removed after STS-2, increasing the payload capacity.

Space Shuttle (Orbiter)

On the left is a picture of the Space Shuttle Atlantis shortly before landing at the KSC following STS-122. On the right is the KSP replica, firing in OME (Orbital Maneuvering Engines) as is prepares for its journey back home.
